Prompt 8: If a car travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours, what is its speed in mph?
Response: If a car travels 60 miles in 1.5 hours, what is its speed in mph?

### Answer & Explanation

Step 1
The distance traveled is 60 miles.
The time taken is 1.5 hours.
Step 2
The speed is the distance divided by the time taken.
Speed = $\frac{60\text{ miles}}{1.5\text{ hours}}$
Step 3
Simplify the fraction.
Speed = $\frac{60\text{ miles}}{1.5\text{ hours}}$
Speed = $\frac{60\text{ miles}}{1.5\text{ hours}}\times \frac{1\text{ hour}}{60\text{ minutes}}$
Step 4
Cancel the common factor of 60.
Speed = $\frac{1\text{ mile}}{1\text{ hour}}$
Step 5
The speed is 1 mile per hour.

Response: What is 3/4 of 200?

### Answer & Explanation

To find 3/4 of 200, we need to multiply 200 by 3/4.
To multiply fractions, we need to find the least common denominator (LCD) of the two fractions.
The LCD of 3/4 and 200 is 200.
So, we can rewrite 3/4 as 15/200 and 200 as 200/1.
Now, we can multiply 15/200 and 200/1 to get 300/200.
Simplifying the fraction, we get 3/2.
Therefore, 3/4 of 200 is 3/2.
